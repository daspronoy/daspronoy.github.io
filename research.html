Flagship projects

Spin-resolved deep-microscopic optical bandstructure in chiral tellurium

Developed a quantum, lattice-resolved framework to compute optical eigenwaves inside a crystal, including momentum-exchange processes with the lattice. This yields a spin-resolved optical bandstructure and exposes hidden optical waves with deep-microscopic spin textures. In the small-momentum limit, the splitting of the dominant optical indicators connects directly to the observed optical gyrotropy, enabling quantitative agreement with experiment.

Quantum theory of orbital angular momentum fluctuations in spatiotemporal optical vortices

Developed a quantum framework for STOVs with arbitrary tilt beyond the paraxial regime, enabling simultaneous transverse and longitudinal OAM components. Identified a uniquely STOV-specific signature: non-vanishing longitudinal OAM noise. Defined the OAM noise density as a spatially and temporally evolving “texture” that can be probed experimentally.

Methods and tooling

Theory stack

Operator-based electrodynamics, response functions, and symmetry constraints, with deliberate attention to where macroscopic approximations fail (spatial dispersion, finite momentum, and unit-cell-scale field structure).

  • • Current-current optical correlation functions with lattice momentum exchange
  • • Nonlocal conductivity and dielectric tensors, eigenmodes and indicators
  • • Quantum photonic angular momentum operators and fluctuations

Computation stack

Scalable numerics for high-dimensional Hamiltonians and response matrices, optimized to make “microscopic” physics computable across broad frequency and momentum ranges.

  • • Sparse eigen-solvers, high-dimensional linear algebra
  • • C++ modules integrated into Purdue-PicoMax; Python/Matlab analysis pipelines
  • • Reproducible workflows for figures, benchmarks, and parameter sweeps

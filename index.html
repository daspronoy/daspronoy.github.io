About

I am a researcher in quantum optics. I explore how light can be shaped: how it twists, spins, and flows in space and time, and how those properties interact with advanced materials. My goal is to turn deep physics into practical capabilities for imaging, sensing, and robust photonic devices.

  • Elmore Family School of Electrical and Computer Engineering
  • Birck Nanotechnology Center
  • Purdue Quantum Science and Engineering Institute
  • Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, USA

Research

Deep-microscopic optics of chiral tellurium

Spin-resolved optical bandstructure; nonlocal optical gyrotropy; hidden optical waves and spin textures revealed inside the lattice, with Purdue-PicoMax tooling.

Optical bandstructure Super-dispersive gyrotropy Hidden optical spin texture Purdue-PicoMax

Quantum OAM in spatiotemporal optical vortices

Spatiotemporal optical vortices beyond the paraxial limit; quantum states (Fock and coherent pulses); texture of OAM fluctuations.

3D OAM noise Fock and coherent pulses Commutation relations (photon AM)

Photon angular momentum fundamentals

Gauge-invariant SAM/OAM decomposition and corrected commutation relations; generators of rotations for observable fields.

SAM/OAM Commutation Observable fields

Quantum metrology with NV centers

Sagnac-based GHz phase and amplitude modulation for spin-textured light; shallow NV creation and cryogenic ODMR instrumentation.

Sagnac interferometry Shallow NV (~30 nm) Cryo ODMR setup

Sensing and plasmonics

Localized spoof surface plasmon resonators for ESR detection down to 10 K; field confinement and cryogenic behavior.

Software: Purdue-PicoMax

Purdue-PicoMax

Open tooling to compute spin-resolved deep-microscopic optical bandstructure and super-dispersive optical gyrotropy from current-current correlations with full lattice momentum-exchange processes. Built for chiral materials such as tellurium and extensible to general crystalline systems.
